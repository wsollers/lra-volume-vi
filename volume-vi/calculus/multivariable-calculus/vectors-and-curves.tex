\subsection*{Vectors and Curves}

\begin{definition*}[Vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) and \(\mathbb{R}^3\)]
Vectors encode magnitude and direction. In \(\mathbb{R}^2\) and
\(\mathbb{R}^3\), they are manipulated through component arithmetic, dot
products, cross products, projections, and parametrized lines and planes.
\end{definition*}

\begin{definition*}[Vector-Valued Function]
A vector-valued function \(\mathbf r(t)\) traces a curve. Its derivative
\(\mathbf r'(t)\) is the velocity vector and points in the tangent direction
when nonzero.
\end{definition*}
