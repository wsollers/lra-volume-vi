\subsection*{Definition}

\begin{definition*}[Derivative as a Difference Quotient Limit]
The derivative of \(f\) at \(a\) is
\[
  f'(a)=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h},
\]
when this limit exists.
\end{definition*}

\begin{remark*}[Slope and Rate of Change]
Geometrically, \(f'(a)\) is the slope of the tangent line to \(y=f(x)\) at
\(x=a\). In applications, it is the instantaneous rate of change of the
output with respect to the input.
\end{remark*}

\begin{example*}[Power from the Definition]
For \(f(x)=x^2\),
\[
  \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}
    =\frac{(a+h)^2-a^2}{h}
    =2a+h,
\]
so \(f'(a)=2a\). This is a short computational derivation, not a theorem
proof vault entry.
\end{example*}
