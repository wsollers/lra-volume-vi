\subsection*{Implicit Differentiation}

\begin{remark*}[Method]
Differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\), treating \(y\) as a function
of \(x\). Every derivative of an expression involving \(y\) picks up a factor
of \(dy/dx\). Then solve algebraically for \(dy/dx\).
\end{remark*}

\begin{example*}[Circle]
From \(x^2+y^2=1\), differentiate to get
\[
  2x+2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0,
  \qquad
  \frac{dy}{dx}=-\frac{x}{y}.
\]
\end{example*}
